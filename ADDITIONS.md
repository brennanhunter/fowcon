What's Missing
1. No "Why Choose Fowcon" section — Kitchen and bathroom pages both have a WhyUs component highlighting custom design, upfront pricing, in-house crews, and licensing. The additions page skips straight from services to FAQs. For a high-ticket project like a home addition ($50K–$200K+), trust-building content is critical. People shopping additions are comparing 3–5 contractors.

2. No gallery / before-and-after photos — Kitchen has 14 projects, bathroom has 12+. Additions has zero. This is the biggest gap — visual proof is what converts on construction pages. Even 3–4 project photos would help while you build the portfolio.

3. No JSON-LD structured data — Kitchen and bathroom pages have both Service and FAQPage schema markup for Google rich results. Additions has neither. Easy SEO win.

4. Only 4 FAQs vs. 5 on sibling pages — Missing questions homeowners commonly ask: rough cost ranges, whether they can live in the home during construction, and how additions affect property value/resale.

What Could Be Stronger
5. Hero has no image — Just a blue background with text. Kitchen/bathroom heroes are more visual. A photo of a completed addition (or even an in-progress shot showing the scale of work) would make a stronger first impression.

6. Services section is thin — 4 cards with ~1 sentence each. Kitchen has 6 cards with more detail. Consider expanding to include things like: in-law suites, home offices (big post-COVID demand), bonus rooms, or bathroom/kitchen additions specifically.

7. Financing FAQ is vague — "We offer flexible options tailored to your needs" vs. bathroom's page which gives more specifics. For additions (the most expensive service), financing details matter the most.

8. No mention of permitting/engineering expertise — Additions are the most permit-heavy, code-intensive service. The FAQ touches on it, but a dedicated callout that Fowcon handles all engineering, permitting, and inspections would differentiate from handyman-type competitors.

What's Too Much / Fine As-Is
The page is actually too lean rather than too much. Nothing needs to be removed — it just needs to be filled out to match the depth of the kitchen/bathroom pages.
